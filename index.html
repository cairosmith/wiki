Cairo Smith (born July 12, 1997) is an American screenwriter, film director, playwright, and author. His debut feature, the psychological thriller Screwdriver (2023), premiered at the Dances With Films festival in Los Angeles before screening at the SOHO International Film Festival, and is currently available on Amazon Prime Video. Smith’s earlier work includes writing and directing narrative experiences for Niantic Labs’ augmented‑reality game Ingress, which served more than twenty million players worldwide. He publishes fiction and essays through his Substack, Futurist Letters, and frequently speaks on the intersection of storytelling and emerging technology.

Early life and education

Smith was born in Berkeley, California, and raised in Los Angeles. He studied film production at California State University, Northridge, where his original play earned the Kennedy Center College Theater Award.

Career

After university, Smith joined Niantic Labs, eventually rising from production assistant to writer‑director on Ingress. His short films screened at regional festivals before he crowdfunded and shot Screwdriver, a feature exploring startup culture and artificial intelligence. The film received positive notices from Film Threat and the Los Angeles Times. In 2025 he completed principal photography on his second feature, Lord of Space.
